Vitamin D deficiency is more widespread than most people realize. It affects nearly 1 billion people worldwide and is especially common in regions with limited sun exposure, older adults, and individuals with certain health conditions.

Signs You Might Be Deficient

Many symptoms are subtle or mistaken for other issues:

  • Frequent illness or infections
  • Fatigue and low energy
  • Bone or muscle pain
  • Mood swings or depression
  • Slow wound healing

Even those who eat healthfully and get some sun exposure may still have low levels due to age, skin tone, medication use, or digestive absorption issues.

High-Risk Groups

  • People living in northern latitudes
  • Older adults
  • Those with darker skin tones
  • People with obesity or digestive conditions
  • Individuals who spend most time indoors or wear full-body coverings

The Impact of Deficiency

Vitamin D plays a vital role in calcium absorption, immune function, and cellular health. Without adequate levels, you may be more prone to:

  • Osteopenia or osteoporosis
  • Autoimmune flare-ups
  • Cardiovascular problems
  • Cognitive decline
