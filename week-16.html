Blog 16: Vitamin D's Role in Reducing Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD)

When the days grow shorter and the skies turn gray, many people notice a dip in mood. This isn't just "winter blues"â€”for some, it's Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD), a type of depression tied directly to light deficiency. One nutrient often missing in the darker months? Vitamin D.

The Sunshine Vitamin and Mood Regulation

Vitamin D is involved in regulating the production of serotonin, the neurotransmitter often referred to as the "happy chemical." During the winter months, reduced sunlight exposure leads to lower Vitamin D synthesis in the skin, which may contribute to feelings of sadness, fatigue, and low motivation.

Researchers have observed a clear correlation between low Vitamin D levels and a higher risk of depression, particularly SAD.

Signs of SAD May Include:

  • Feeling tired despite adequate sleep
  • Loss of interest or pleasure in activities
  • Irritability or anxiety
  • Cravings for carbs or weight gain
