Beyond Bones: A Full-Body Benefit

Vitamin D's most recognized role is in calcium regulation and bone density. However, it also supports immune defense, muscle strength, cognitive clarity, and even cardiovascular health. As we age, these systems begin to slow down, making Vitamin D more important than ever.

Age-Related Deficiency Risks

The body's ability to synthesize Vitamin D from sunlight decreases with age. This is compounded by less time outdoors and decreased dietary absorption. For older adults, deficiency is linked to:

  • Increased fall risk
  • Weaker immune response
  • Slower wound healing
  • Bone thinning and fractures
  • Cognitive decline

Fall Prevention and Muscle Health

Several studies show that Vitamin D supplementation reduces fall risk in older adults by improving muscle strength and neuromuscular control. Falls are a leading cause of injury and hospitalization in older populationsâ€”so supporting balance and stability is vital.
